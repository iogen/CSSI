Childhood

Jeremie's family relocated when he was at the age of three. He grew up hearing a multitude of languages. Six in total, but seldom remembers these langauges. Living in Germany for the first 3 years of his life he was filled with German. However, he grew to hate Spanish and French the more his parents regularly spoke it. Completly the opposite of his older siblings who still can speak French and Spanish. After relocating to the United States at three, he also had very short briefs living stints in the city of Atlanta, New York, Chicago, and Dallas. He never claims them because living for less than a year does not give you the right to truly say you lived there. They finally settled down in St. Louis at the age of 6. It wasn't until he was 15 years old that he revisited Nigeria and reconnected with family members he has never seen before.

Activities

Chess

As Chess Team Co-Captain, Jeremie has shared the knowledge of Chess to hundreds of students in his school. As one of five members to represent his school in club tournaments, he has helped his team to make it to state in the first year. He was the most improved player, who started off as the worst player his coach stated he has ever seen. He ended up being the only student to beat the coach in all his years of coaching.

National Organization of Community Care

Seeing the disparity in the homeless, Jim and Jeremie started National Organization of Community Care to provide basic necessities to the homeless during the holiday seasons. The past Thanksgiving season was their best season yet, fundraising to feed over 350 homeless through the Hazelwood food pantry, they provided a Thanksgiving meal for those who didn’t have it.

Botany Ecological-Engineering

Along with his friend Michael, Jeremie founded Botany Ecological-Engineering club, to raise the awareness of environmental issues in his community. He planted flowers and reconstruct an entire outdoor classroom amphitheater for improved learning. Along with others he planned the building of the greenhouse that would protect the foods they planted and donated to the local homeless shelter. His love for teaching was shown by partnering and volunteering with elementary schools to teach mini labs about the environment to improve the enrollment of STEM in younger children.

Mercy Hospital

Jeremie’s love for helping others doesn’t stop there. Being and active volunteer at a Mercy Hospital, he has gone through many floor of the hospital which include, Pediatrics,Psych Ward, Burn Unit, Pathology, Transitional Care Unit, and the Heart Hospital. Assisting in rehabilitation and improvement of prognosis of patients, Jeremie records, collects, and helps nurse the patients back to full health.
